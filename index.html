SemantEx is a contraction of Semantic Extraction extracting meaning from text.

The system was initially designed to extract meaning and use it, so it is perhaps ironic that it also turns out to be very useful to extract meaning and throw it away...

Time is Money

Save the time, save the money, get the SemantEx AI redaction system to do it for you.

On top of everything else your team has to cope with - along comes GDPR.

Now you have to supply documents for an employment tribunal - GDPR insists that you have to remove all references to third parties from the documents; names, initials, email addresses, and the rest.

A search of your archive reveals you have over a thousand pages of emails, reports and meeting notes. That could take one of your para-legals or hard pressed HR staff more than 2 days work, but not unusual for an employee who has been with you for 2 years.

The SemantEx system will automatically remove all names other than that specified as the 'subject' as well as initials, emails and other information types you specify.

AI reads text in a manner as similar as possible to a human, using grammar and the rules of association, possession, incorporation and many others to understand the meaning of the text.

It employs background knowledge extracted from dictionaries and encyclopedias which it builds on and refines as it acquires further knowledge.

It is this process of knowledge acquisition through which the system learns unline the statistical processes underpinning neural nets and 'deep learning' systems

As good as a human?

How good is a human? Humans get bored, tired and stressed.

A long document redaction will contain errors and will need checking.

The SemantEx AI system may also miss things - but with an accuracy of over 95%, it seems to outperform most humans.

It may still need checking, but the checking process is typically fast - the few missed items tend to stand out.

The system is also getting better all the time and there is the flexibility to add terms to the redaction that the system does not know.

Names can also be 'white-listed' if they must not be redacted. The AI also has the capability to remove adjectives that might give things away (think "big" in "big John") and associated references.
